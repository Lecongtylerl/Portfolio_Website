1 Setup Screen
Emma opens the game and sees plain white buttons on a light gray background. The layout is clean with theme and difficulty options clearly labeled. However, it looks like a settings page rather than a game. There is nothing visually exciting to engage a young player.
2 Gameplay
Emma flips cards on a 3×4 grid. All unflipped cards are identical gray squares, making it hard to feel any progress. Matched pairs just get a line-through on the letter — easy to miss while scanning. Two cards are currently flipped but the reveal feedback is flat with no animation or excitement.
3 Game Over
Emma wins and sees a plain “Game Complete” heading with her stats in boxes. There is no celebration, no animation, and no visual reward. The screen feels like finishing a form rather than winning a game. She has little motivation to replay.
Design Notes: This storyboard uses a white/gray palette, Arial system font, and minimal blue accents. Strengths: text is readable, layout is clear and easy to scan, familiar UI patterns. Weaknesses: no game personality or visual identity, the generic look could be any app, match feedback is too subtle (line-through text), the win screen lacks any excitement or celebration, overall design would not engage a young user like Emma.

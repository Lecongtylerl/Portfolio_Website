1 Setup Screen
Robert sees a terminal-style interface with glowing cyan text. His high scores are displayed in a neon panel. However, the tiny neon text on the pure black background is hard to read. Unselected buttons are nearly invisible, and the “EXECUTE” label feels uninviting for a casual game.
2 Gameplay
Robert plays on Hard mode (5×4 grid). Matched cards glow green which stands out, but the unflipped cards are nearly invisible against the black background. Scanning the grid for untried positions is straining on his eyes. The abbreviated stat labels (T, M, EST) save space but hurt clarity.
3 Game Over
Robert beats his previous best score. The “NEW_RECORD” banner is present but small and cold. The dark screen with neon glow has no warmth or celebration — it feels like a system log, not a personal achievement. Score comparison is functional but not satisfying.
Design Notes: This storyboard uses a black background, cyan/magenta neon accents, and Courier monospace font inside each screen. Strengths: strong game identity, matched cards are clearly distinct with green glow, score tracking is always visible. Weaknesses: dark background causes eye strain during extended play, neon text is hard to read for older users, unflipped cards nearly disappear, the cold terminal aesthetic feels impersonal and uninviting for a casual memory game.
